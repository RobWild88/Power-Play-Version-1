📖 POWER PLAY — Rules

🎯 The Goal

Capture the enemy Commander-in-Chief (CiC). Gray Army moves first.

🗺️ The Board

  • Red Zone (back row) — your home base
  • Blue Zone (second row) — your front line
  • White Zone (middle rows) — the battlefield

♟️ The Pieces

  • Foot Soldier — moves forward 1 space (or 2 from starting row). Captures diagonally. Only 1 space per turn in enemy territory.
  • Army Officer — slides any number of squares horizontally or vertically. Two Officers facing each other with nothing between form a Barrier.
  • Air Force — moves in an L-shape (2+1 squares). Jumps over other pieces. Two Air Force pieces one L-jump apart become Linked and invincible for 3 turns.
  • Navy — slides diagonally any distance, OR shifts 1 square straight.
  • General — moves any direction, any distance. Can also do L-jumps if the path is clear.
  • Commander-in-Chief — moves 1 square in any direction. Protect this piece!

⚠️ Special Rules

  • Challenge — If your CiC is threatened, a ⚠️ CHALLENGE alert appears. You must move out of danger immediately.
  • Barrier — Two of your Army Officers aligned on the same row or column (nothing between them) block enemies from crossing that line. Planes ignore barriers.
  • Plane Link — Two Air Force pieces one L-jump apart become LINKED — they cannot be captured for 3 of the opponent's turns.
  • Recruiting — When a Foot Soldier reaches the enemy Red Zone, choose: recruit a captured piece back onto the board, or earn a Power Play token.

⚡ Special Abilities (once per game each)

  • ⚡ Power Play — Move up to 3 different pieces in one turn (max 2 Army Officers). Each piece may only be moved once during the Power Play. Cannot capture the enemy Commander-in-Chief — at most put them in Challenge. Cannot use while in Challenge.
  • 🌊 Navigate — Instantly move one of your Navy pieces to any open square in your Blue Zone.
  • 🛡️ Defend — When your CiC and an Army Officer are both in your Red Zone, reposition both anywhere within your Red Zone.
